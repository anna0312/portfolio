Go There

Go There is a round-the-world travel planning site built as a final project for my WDI course at General Assembly.

Go There was inspired by my passion for travel and a plan to travel the circumference of the world with my husband. There are many travel sites out there, but I didn't find one that enabled you to plan a multi-legged journey and access the data to make decisions about where to go, when to go there, and what order to go there in.

The site was built in 5 days using technologies that were new to my skill stack and as a great way to put what I learned to work. This is an on-going project with features that I am continuing to flesh out. Stay tuned for updates!

Technologies used: Node.js, Express, MongoDB, Mongoose, HTML/CSS, JavaScript

Integrations and plug-ins used: Google Maps, Dragula, Bootstrap Notify,

Cupid's Undie Run

my.cupids.org is the fundraising and participant portal for Cupid's Undie Run, a charity event raing money for the Children's Tumor Foundation.

While at Blue Sky Collaborative, we started working with Cupid's Charities in 2012, when the Cupid's Undie Run was in its early stages and had rasied $50,000. They were looking for a firm that could provide them with a customized fundraising component that would fit their unique needs and expand with them. They have since grown dramatically and raise several million dollars a year and hold events accross the country with tens of thousands of participants.

Over the years, the my.cupids.org site has evolved to contain many cutting edge fundraising tools, including:

  • Participant registration
  • Online donations with multiple payment option types
  • Donation matching
  • Participant fundraising incentive tracking
  • Volunteer management
  • Multiple levels of site administration and reporting
  • Customizeable content
  • Built-in email marketing
  • Referral bonus management
  • Photo uploaders

Technologies used:ASP, SQL Server, Javascript, HTML/CSS

Integrations: Authorize.net, PayPal, ActiveCampaign, Cloudesponge, Facebook, Tableau, MailChimp, Seko

Bike to the Beach

my.biketothebeach.org is the fundraising portal site used by Bike to the Beach, a charity bike ride that raises money for Autism.

Bike to the Beach came to us with a request similar to what Cupid's was looking for: a fundraising and event-management portal that accomodates their unique event registration logic special features they wanted to provide their riders with.

Special features include:

  • Registration with referal and promo codes
  • Badges earned by participants through various acheivements
  • Incentive points earned by participants that can be cashed in for registration payment discounts and to use at their shopify store.
  • Multi-legged ride-long volunteer sign up and management
  • Fully integrated email system
  • A complex integration with Salesforce that breaks data down into several tiers including donors, teams, participants, and rides.

Technologies used:ASP, SQL Server, Javascript, HTML/CSS

Integrations: Salesforce, FirstData, PayEezy, Strava, Mailchimp, Cloudesponge
